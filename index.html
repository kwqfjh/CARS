Why Electric Cars Aren't The Way Forward

Why Electric Cars Are Bad for the Environment

1. Battery Production Pollution

Electric car batteries require lithium, cobalt, and nickel, mined in ways that damage ecosystems. Mining operations can pollute rivers and destroy habitats.

2. Energy Source Issues

Many electric cars are still charged using coal or gas-powered electricity. The carbon footprint of charging can sometimes match or exceed petrol car emissions over the vehicle’s lifetime.

3. End-of-Life Waste

Battery disposal is a growing problem. Old lithium-ion batteries are hard to recycle and can leak toxic chemicals into the environment.

Benefits of Petrol Cars

1. Lower Production Footprint

Petrol cars don’t require massive batteries. Producing a petrol car emits less pollution upfront compared to building an electric vehicle.

2. Fueling Infrastructure & Convenience

Petrol stations are everywhere, reducing energy losses from home charging and transport. Quick refueling reduces energy waste and keeps vehicles on the road longer.

3. Performance & Longevity

Petrol engines provide consistent performance and can last decades if maintained. Less dependence on rare metals means fewer environmental and ethical issues.
